Elevate

ELEVATE TEST is a dynamic 4-year initiative designed to strengthen the IADT Research and Innovation Office. ELEVATE’s primary goal is to cultivate a dynamic research and innovation ecosystem for the Creative Industries, propelling Ireland to the forefront of the sector and solidifying IADT as a global leader in creative research and innovation. With the support from the TU Rise programme, our initial emphasis will focus on Technology and Policy for the Creative industries, bolstered by overarching themes of sustainable innovation and Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion (EDI).

IADT’s TU RISE ELEVATE is co-financed by the Government of Ireland and the European Union through the ERDF Southern, Eastern & Midland Regional Programme 2021- 27 and the Northern & Western Regional Programme 2021-27

The overall motivation of the ELEVATE project aligns the scope and outcomes of TU RISE and European and National strategies as it seeks to establish and grow dedicated research and innovation offices, amply research and innovation for the creative industries and broaden IADTs engagement with regional enterprises, with a particular focus on SMEs.

Objectives of the programme

  • Develop Regional Creative Industries Research and Innovation Ecosystem
  • Strengthen IADT’s Research Capacity and Expertise
  • Expand IADT’s Pathway for Research Outputs into Industry
  • Develop and Improve Support Structures for Research and Industry Engagement
  • Promote Equality, Diversity and Inclusion

“Work with Stakeholder groups and produce research that impacts society, bridges knowledge and expertise and its translation to industry and the regions and find creative solutions for the most pressing cultural and societal issues pertinent to our shared future.”

Extending our specialist research focus, four “Creation Bridges” will transform our campus into a “live studio” in these areas:

A. Creation Bridge SCREEN – Growing IADT’s existing expertise in Animation, Expanded Screen Practices, Cyberpsychology, Creative Computing, AR/VR, Immersive Media Technologies, Gaming/Digital Content and Production.

B. Creation Bridge DESIGN – consolidate IADT’s expertise in Communication, Information, Strategic and Organisational Design, 3D Design, UI/UX, Spatial, Speculative, Environmental Design, Visual Arts, Critical Design Approaches, Design Thinking and Artistic Research.

C. Creation Bridge POLICY – Promoting evidence-based research, informing and shaping political and civic debate on the social and economic impacts of policy on the Creative and Cultural Sector and Industries (CCSI), culture and society, through the lens of EDI, human interaction, the Social Sciences, thought Leadership and Strategic Planning. This virtuous RDI ecosystem is fully integrated with our strategy, enabling IADT to place research at the epicentre of its activities. It reflects IADT ethos and vision which is guided by the transformative potential for creative and critical practices to release original ideas about how we understand the world now, and in the future. It capacitates IADT to deliver on its stated mission to re-model and revolutionise the future of the Creative Industries.

D. Creation Bridge GROW — is the research growth, training, engagement and impact engine, which will host our academics, teams of researchers, and a suite of training packages, and develop networks for our Academic Residency and Industry Fellowship programmes to seed and grow the next areas of specialisations.

These bridges are gateways and generators to other areas of research specialisation and expertise, and they coalesce as a force for positive social change.

Ethical screen practices and responsible content creation produce beneficial outcomes for society, forging pathways to a more cohesive future. Critical design methodologies lever imaginative and daring problem-solving approaches to the biggest challenges facing society, such as Climate Change. Creative approaches to leadership produce an informed and persuasive model for policy making, impacting significant challenges facing the CCSI in such areas as EDI and Sustainability. The foundation for nurturing, and shaping the above, is IADT’s demonstrable excellence in Psychology, Technology and Critical Humanities research in Art, Design, Performance, Screen and Textual Histories and Theories, Visual, Material, Public and Popular Cultures. This transdisciplinary, interdisciplinary, and profoundly collaborative ecosystem is powered by the multifaceted and nuanced approaches emancipated when rigorous creative practices and critical research combine.

ELEVATE includes a diverse array of research and industry engagement initiatives including:

  • Creative Industries Analysis Mapping the creative economy of the Southern Eastern Midlands. Development of a strategic plan for engaging industry partners. Introduction of a Toolkit for Research in the Creative Industries.
  • Research Leadership Development Recruitment of Postgraduates, Postdocs and Research Leaders Validation of one 5 credit Special Purpose Award (Level 9) in Cultural Entrepreneurship. Provision of bespoke training and mentoring for academic staff and researchers.
  • Innovate Vision Lab The establishment of a cutting-edge Innovation Vision Laboratory with dedicated technical support. The Innovation Vision Lab will facilitate collaboration between researchers and industry partners utilising state of the art audio visual technology.
  • ELEVATE Events ELEVATE will deliver a series of events designed to initiate and nurture industry/academia collaboration. These will include showcase events, student events, the IADT Accelerator programme and two events focusing upon female founders in the creative and cultural industries.
  • Equality, Diversity and Inclusion ELEVATE will develop a conscious and inclusive approach to research and research culture through strengthening understanding of equality, diversity, inclusivity and belonging amongst our IADT community.

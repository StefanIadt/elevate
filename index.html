Elevate

ELEVATE is a dynamic 4-year initiative designed to strengthen the IADT Research and Innovation Office. ELEVATE’s primary goal is to cultivate a dynamic research and innovation ecosystem for the Creative Industries, propelling Ireland to the forefront of the sector and solidifying IADT as a global leader in creative research and innovation. With the support from the TU Rise programme, our initial emphasis will focus on Technology and Policy for the Creative industries, bolstered by overarching themes of sustainable innovation and Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion (EDI).

IADT’s TU RISE ELEVATE is co-financed by the Government of Ireland and the European Union through the ERDF Southern, Eastern & Midland Regional Programme 2021- 27 and the Northern & Western Regional Programme 2021-27

The overall motivation of the ELEVATE project aligns the scope and outcomes of TU RISE and European and National strategies as it seeks to establish and grow dedicated research and innovation offices, amply research and innovation for the creative industries and broaden IADTs engagement with regional enterprises, with a particular focus on SMEs.

Programme Objectives

  1. Develop Regional Creative Industries Research and Innovation Ecosystem
  2. Strengthen IADT’s Research Capacity and Expertise
  3. Expand IADT’s Pathway for Research Outputs into Industry
  4. Develop and Improve Support Structures for Research and Industry Engagement
  5. Promote Equality, Diversity and Inclusion

Work with Stakeholder groups and produce research that impacts society, bridges knowledge and expertise and its translation to industry and the regions and find creative solutions for the most pressing cultural and societal issues pertinent to our shared future.

Creation Bridges will transform our campus into a “live studio” in these areas:

Creation Bridge: Screen

Growing IADT’s existing expertise in Animation, Expanded Screen Practices, Cyberpsychology, Creative Computing, AR/VR, Immersive Media Technologies, Gaming/Digital Content and Production.

Creation Bridge: Design

Consolidate IADT’s expertise in Communication, Information, Strategic and Organisational Design, 3D Design, UI/UX, Spatial, Speculative, Environmental Design, Visual Arts, Critical Design Approaches, Design Thinking and Artistic Research.

Creation Bridge: Policy

Promoting evidence-based research to inform debate on policy impacts in the Creative and Cultural Sector through EDI, Social Sciences, and Strategic Planning. Integrated with IADT’s strategy, this ecosystem supports its vision of transformative creative practices, enabling IADT to revolutionise the future of Creative Industries.

Creation Bridge: Grow

Is the research growth, training, engagement and impact engine, which will host our academics, teams of researchers, and a suite of training packages, and develop networks for our Academic Residency and Industry Fellowship programmes to seed and grow the next areas of specialisations.

These bridges are gateways and generators to other areas of research specialisation and expertise, and they coalesce as a force for positive social change.

Ethical screen practices and responsible content creation produce beneficial outcomes for society, forging pathways to a more cohesive future. Critical design methodologies lever imaginative and daring problem-solving approaches to the biggest challenges facing society, such as Climate Change. Creative approaches to leadership produce an informed and persuasive model for policy making, impacting significant challenges facing the CCSI in such areas as EDI and Sustainability.

The foundation for nurturing, and shaping the above, is IADT’s demonstrable excellence in Psychology, Technology and Critical Humanities research in Art, Design, Performance, Screen and Textual Histories and Theories, Visual, Material, Public and Popular Cultures. This transdisciplinary, interdisciplinary, and profoundly collaborative ecosystem is powered by the multifaceted and nuanced approaches emancipated when rigorous creative practices and critical research combine.

Dr Rachel Clarke

Programme Manager ELEVATE

Dr Rachel Clarke is programme manager for the ELEVATE programme. ELEVATE is funded under the TU Research and Innovation Supporting Enterprise scheme (TU RISE). Prior to joining IADT, Rachel was previously employed by the Irish Research Council (IRC) where she managed a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) programme in public health crises and a digital humanities programme co-funded by the Arts and Humanities Research Council, UK and the IRC.

Rachel Gallagher

Enterprise & Innovation Manager

Rachel Gallagher is Enterprise & Innovation Manager at the Institute of Art, Design and Technology (IADT). Rachel manages the IADT incubator centre, The Media Cube, and Enterprise Ireland’s New Frontiers programme team at IADT (an entrepreneurial development programme for early-stage start-ups). In addition, Rachel manages a suite of extra-curricular student entrepreneurship programmes, including a student bootcamp and accelerator programme.
